What the numbers are saying
Enrollment cliff
First-year applications fell ~25% year over year, in line with the national demographic downturn now reaching the 2025 cohort. Watch deposits, not just applications.
Funnel
As the pool shrank, the admit rate rose to 51% while yield still ticked up to 21%. The class is holding on fewer, better-fit applicants.
Concentration risk
Graduate is 48% international with source countries clustered, India the largest. A single visa-policy shift becomes an enrollment event.

Why did undergraduate applications drop this year, and should we be worried?

Undergraduate first-year applications came in at 10,670, down from 14,170 the prior cycle. Two forces are stacking here.

Applications, year over year
▼ 24.7%
Yield, year over year
▲ 3.8 pts

The demographic cliff. The number of graduating high-school seniors is shrinking nationally, and 2025 is the first steep step down. This is sector wide, not specific to Meridian.

The easy-apply bump faded. Test-optional policy drove a surge in 2021 and 2022. As that normalized, the pool got smaller but more intentional.

The reassuring part: as the pool shrank, yield still rose to 21.0% and the admit rate climbed to 51%. You are converting a higher share of a more committed pool. For the rest of the cycle, watch deposits and melt rather than raw application counts.
