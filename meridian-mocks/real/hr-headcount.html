Has our workforce kept pace with the student population over the last decade?

Short answer, no. Total employee headcount fell from 1,438 in 2015 to 1,047 in 2024, a decline of about 27%, while the combined fall active student count rose over much of the same stretch before a 2024 drop.

Headcount, 2015 to 2024
▼ 27.2%
Staff part time share, 2024
▲ 36.0%

The mix shifted. Faculty full time stayed thin across the decade while staff part time grew to roughly a third of the workforce by 2024. That points to a leaner, more flexible staffing model.

Watch the gap. Graduate enrollment held strong through 2023, then fell sharply in 2024 even as undergraduates stayed near 4,176. If headcount keeps shrinking, service ratios per student tighten further.
